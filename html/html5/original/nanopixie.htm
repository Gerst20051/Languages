nanopixie

There are thousands of content management systems available and in the past ten years they haven't changed that much. On top of that most of them are a real pain to setup and use. It's about time we saw an intuitive way to update the Internet, with less forms and less fuss.

What's a Pixie?

In May 2008 I launched an Open Source project to solve this problem. It's called Pixie and I like to think of it as a small, simple website maker. Pixie has done me proud but he is starting to show his age. It is time we found a new way to update the web and HTML5 may hold the answer.

HTML5

With the HTML5 specification comes the standardisation of a feature known as contentEditable. contentEditable allows client side in-browser rich text editing and all major browsers now support this (including Internet Explorer, since 5.5).

How this works

Use the toolbar at the top of the screen to enter edit mode (). Once pressed the edit buttons will come alive and you can begin manipulating this page. Press save () and a copy of the page will be saved to your computer using localStorage.
